About Presto-Build

This project is just getting underway, so there isn’t much here yet. But if you’re interested in helping out, give me a shout.

Presto - Etymology: Italian, quick, quickly, from Latin praestus ready; used to indicate the sudden appearance or occurrence of something often as if by magic. — Merriam-Webster

Presto-Build is a tool that is used to build other programs, akin to GNU Make. Much like Make, Presto-Build reads a Makefile that describes how to build your program, and how various pieces of your program are related. From that description, Presto-Build will rebuild the target without requiring that the user know the precise details.

Presto-Build was born out of my frustration with trying to accomplish certain advanced tasks using Make. Presto-Build is heavily inspired by Make. I have always been very fond of the simplicity and elegance of its underlying design, and Presto-Build aims to preserve that elegance. At the same time, I hope to offering a number of compelling features that would be difficult or impossible to achieve using Make.

Presto-Build is built directly on top of the Lua Programming Language. Like Make, Lua has a reputation for simplicity and elegance in its design—as well as its speed of execution! All of Presto-Build's makefiles are actually Lua scripts, and the full power of Lua scripting is available when crafting your build system.
